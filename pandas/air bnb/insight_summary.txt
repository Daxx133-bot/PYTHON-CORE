NYC AIRBNB — INSIGHT SUMMARY
============================================================

▶ DATA QUALITY
  • Dataset Overview
    The dataset contains 48,884 Airbnb listings across 5 NYC boroughs with 23 attributes. Data completeness is 98.2%. There are 16 numeric and 7 categorical columns.

▶ PRICING ANALYTICS
  • Price Distribution & Market Segmentation
    Nightly prices range from $10 to $10,000 with a median of $106 and mean of $153 (heavily right-skewed). 31.2% of listings are budget (≤$75/night) while 6.9% are luxury (>$300/night). The IQR spans $69–$175, indicating a fragmented market with wide price dispersion.

  • Borough-Level Price Hierarchy
    Manhattan commands the highest median nightly rate ($150) — 2.3× more than Bronx ($65). Brooklyn is the second most expensive borough, reflecting its growing popularity as a Manhattan alternative. The Bronx and Staten Island represent value segments with the lowest rates.

▶ MARKET STRUCTURE
  • Room Type Market Composition
    Entire home/apt listings dominate at 52.0% of supply, followed by private rooms (45.7%). Shared rooms are a niche segment at 2.4%. 'Entire home/apt' commands a 2.3× price premium over private rooms. This split signals both leisure travellers seeking privacy and budget guests willing to share spaces.

▶ OPERATIONAL ANALYTICS
  • Listing Availability & Occupancy Signals
    Median annual availability is 45 days, with mean 113 days. 35.9% of listings show zero availability (possibly blocked/inactive), while 2.6% are available year-round. Low availability often correlates with high demand, suggesting popular listings are consistently booked.

▶ GUEST ENGAGEMENT
  • Review Volume & Guest Engagement
    20.6% of listings have zero reviews — potentially new or inactive. Listings with 50+ reviews (14.5%) represent proven, high-engagement properties. Median review count is 5 with mean 23.3. Review count and price show a Pearson r = -0.064, suggesting reviews are not price-driven.

▶ HOST ANALYTICS
  • Host Portfolio Scale & Professionalisation
    66.1% of listings come from solo hosts (1 listing), indicating a large hobbyist/occasional-host segment. However, 9.8% of listings are from hosts with 6+ properties, suggesting meaningful professional operator presence. The most prolific host manages 327 listings — a full commercial operation.

  • Commercial vs Solo Host Pricing Strategy
    Commercial hosts price 1.59× higher than solo hosts on median, suggesting different value propositions. Commercial operators likely benefit from economies of scale in property management, while solo hosts may price conservatively to attract first reviews. This has implications for platform trust and regulatory scrutiny.

▶ GEOGRAPHIC ANALYTICS
  • Top Neighbourhoods by Listing Volume
    The five most listed neighbourhoods are: Williamsburg (3,919), Bedford-Stuyvesant (3,710), Harlem (2,658), Bushwick (2,462), Upper West Side (1,971). Williamsburg and Bedford-Stuyvesant in Brooklyn lead in volume, reflecting the borough's rising popularity. The most expensive neighbourhood by median price is Fort Wadsworth ($800/night).

  • Geographic Density & Supply Hotspots
    Manhattan has the highest listing density with 21,660 listings, followed by Brooklyn (20,095). These two boroughs together represent 85.4% of all NYC supply. Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island are significantly underserved, representing potential growth markets for hosts and investors.

▶ REGULATORY & STRATEGY
  • Minimum Night Requirements & Stay Strategy
    26.0% of listings accept 1-night stays, maximising flexibility. 9.2% require 30+ nights, effectively targeting monthly renters (possibly bypassing short-term rental regulations). Long-stay listings median at $130/night vs $90 for 1-night minimums. Extreme outliers (minimum nights >365) likely represent data errors.

▶ FINANCIAL ANALYTICS
  • Revenue Concentration & Earning Potential
    Using price × availability as a revenue proxy, the top 10% of listings account for 54.4% of estimated total platform revenue — a classic Pareto-style concentration. Median estimated annual revenue is $4,300. Manhattan generates the highest estimated total revenue among all boroughs.

▶ STATISTICAL FINDINGS
  • Statistical Price Difference Across Room Types
    One-way ANOVA confirms that nightly prices differ significantly across room types (F = 1715.41, p = 0.00e+00). Entire home/apt listings are statistically more expensive than private and shared rooms. This validates using room type as a key price predictor in any forecasting model.

▶ ANOMALY DETECTION
  • Anomalous Pricing Detection
    IQR-based outlier detection flagged 2,972 listings (6.1%) with anomalous nightly prices. Outlier listings average $659/night vs $120 for standard listings. These may represent luxury penthouses, data entry errors, or placeholder listings. Excluding them reduces mean price significantly and improves model accuracy.
